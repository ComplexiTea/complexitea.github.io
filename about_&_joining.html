ComplexiTea Research Group

Project overview, how to join, and prerequisites.

What is ComplexiTea?

ComplexiTea is a small research group focused on theoretical computer science and complexity, with a strong emphasis on understanding real systems through models of computation, algorithmic complexity, and information theory.

We meet regularly to read papers, design and analyze models, and build small experimental projects that connect theory with practice (reverse engineering, cryptography, verification, security).

How to join

We are looking for a small group of motivated people who are ready to invest time and energy into learning, reading, and building things together.

  1. Check the prerequisites below and make sure they match your profile.
  2. Prepare a short introduction about you and your interests.
  3. Join the Signal group and briefly present yourself to the group.

We aim for a collaborative atmosphere where everyone reads, presents, and contributes on a regular basis.

Prerequisites

This is not an introductory group. You should already be comfortable with core concepts in computer science and be willing to dig into demanding material.

  • Solid programming skills in at least one language (e.g. C/C++, Rust, Python).
  • Familiarity with basic complexity theory (P, NP, reductions, asymptotic analysis).
  • Comfortable reading and discussing technical material in English.
  • Genuine interest in security, cryptography, reverse engineering, or theoretical CS.
  • Ability to dedicate roughly 5-10 hours per week to reading, thinking, and experiments.
